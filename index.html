What it covers

OpsGate locks down the boring but critical parts of WordPress ops.

Plugin & Core Security

Automated plugin and core scans, wired into your existing WordPress stack. Critical issues are surfaced and tracked in the evidence pack.

  • Track vulnerable plugins across multiple sites.
  • See what was blocked or patched in the last period.
SSL & Domain Monitoring

Keep an eye on certificate expiry and DNS/URL wiring. OpsGate catches the obvious "site is down" problems before your clients do.

  • Expiry horizon alerts (e.g., 7, 30, 60 days).
  • Evidence that you checked — not just a ping tool.
Backups with Proof

Instead of "we think backups are running", OpsGate logs when full site backups were made, where they live, and whether they passed basic health checks.

  • Backups catalogued in MANIFEST.json.
  • Summary in your monthly client PDF.
FTP Guardrails

Watch for risky uploads and enforce block-only policies in front of your WordPress stack. Suspect files are blocked and logged with context.

  • "Block-only" mode: deny risky uploads, log everything.
  • Future: quarantine mode for deeper review.

Questions

FAQ

Is OpsGate SaaS or self-hosted?
Today, OpsGate is designed to run in a self-hosted lab or VPS environment using Docker. The current focus is a WordPress-first ops prototype proving the concept with agencies and SMBs. A managed SaaS offering can follow once the pilot pattern is locked.
Will OpsGate change my existing WordPress hosting setup?
No. OpsGate sits "around" your existing hosting. It talks to WordPress, your FTP/cPanel environment, and your SSL endpoints, but it doesn't force you to re-host on a specific platform.
What's the pricing model?
For the pilot phase, pricing is intentionally simple: a flat pilot fee per batch of sites, then a per-site or per-bundle subscription once you're in production. The exact numbers will depend on your agency size and how many sites you want covered.
Does this replace our existing security plugins?
No — OpsGate isn't another monolithic WordPress plugin. It's an ops and evidence layer. You can keep using your favourite security plugins; OpsGate focuses on orchestrating checks and giving you a single, auditable story of what actually happened.
